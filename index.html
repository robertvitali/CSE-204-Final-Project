The Science

Government Programs

Nearly every state has a cover crop program endorsed by that respective state’s government. This is primarily because everyone benefits from the implementation of cover crops. In the long run, farmers save money because they don’t need to purchase fertilizer, it better replenishes nutrients deep in the soil and cover crops are also known to increase crop yield over time. State governments are incentivized because they save money from decreased erosion, costing the U.S. $44 billion dollars annually. Economics

Ken Rulon is one of five fifth-generation owners of a 6,100-acre family farm in Arcadia, Indiana that is part of Rulon Enterprises LLC. Outlined in the data graphic is the economics behind his choice to plant exclusively cover-crops since 1991. It is important to note Rulon has not tilled his land since 1991, yet his fall 2017 ROI is above 250%. Most impressively, his crops showed tremendous drought-resistance.
